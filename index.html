Acceptance.

Getting ready to deploy a new release of your software?

I hope that the QA team - if you have one - found all of the bugs. Do you remember the last time that a bug got through to production? It wasn't that long ago, probably. What did you do?
  • Interrupt all of your programmers so that they could help find the issue?
  • Revert to the previous release?
  • Panic?
  • All of the above?

Oh, we hate it when that happens.

Look: the automated testing workflows in your CI/CD pipeline - if you have one - are awesome. Statistically, they catch 40-80% of the bugs that your developers are introducing to your system. Win! But what about the other 20-60% of bugs? Isn't something missing?

YES! Humans are missing. Real, live humans who click on things & make dumb data entry errors. They're not part of your pipeline...

But they should be, right? Someone - other than the developer who swears it worked for them - should observe the correct behavior of your changes.

That's what we're here for. When you use Acceptance, we'll track your change requests (via GitHub, GitLab, &/or Bitbucket), and - when you're ready to test - we'll spin up a server (on AWS or DigitalOcean) for your testing (dis)pleasure. When you're done, just click a button to stop the server, leave a little feedback, and approve or disapprove of the change. The request can't be merged until it's approved.
